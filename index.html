How He Approaches This Kind of Story

A lot of crypto crime writing repeats the same numbers without explaining what they mean. Osato tends to do the opposite. He takes a Chainalysis figure, a court filing, or a sanctions list, and works out why a reader should care. He has written the same Tornado Cash story for crypto natives following the legal fight and for ordinary readers trying to understand why a piece of code got sanctioned. That range is the whole job.

A Few Lines From the Actual Articles
No buzzwords, just the numbers and what they mean.
On a minister going on record
National security concerns drove Nigeria's fight with Binance, the minister says. The exchange is facing multiple criminal cases in Nigeria. The government wants to fine Binance $81 billion.

Three short sentences carry the source, the stakes, and the number, in that order.

On Europol and physical crime
That warning has proven true with wrench attacks up more than 54 percent in 2025 compared to last year. Gruesome attacks have happened in France, Sweden, and Norway and also outside the eurozone in Brazil, Canada, and Thailand.

An abstract statistic becomes concrete the moment it gets a list of real places attached to it.

On a smart contract bug
On Monday, DeFi protocol Yearn Finance suffered a $9 million exploit that affected the project's yETH liquid staking pool token. Yearn lost $22 million in two previous flash loan exploits.

Gives the new number and immediately anchors it against the protocol's own history, so a reader can judge how bad this one actually is.
